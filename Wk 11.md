# 1. Software Organizations

## Purpose of a Software Company

### Definition

The primary purpose of a software company is to **provide value to users through software products and services.**

A successful software company requires collaboration between multiple teams—not just software developers. 

---

# 2. Roles in a Software Organization

## A. Marketing Team

### Responsibilities

* Identify market opportunities.
* Conduct market research.
* Identify target customers.
* Understand customer needs.

**Goal:** Find problems worth solving. 

---

## B. Product Manager

### Definition

A **Product Manager (PM)** connects **Business, Technology, and User Experience (UX)**.

The Venn diagram in the lecture illustrates the PM at the intersection of these three domains. 

### Responsibilities

* Work with marketing and sales.
* Understand business goals.
* Maximize return on investment (ROI).
* Understand the technology stack.
* Estimate development effort.
* Make technical decisions.
* Keep the user's needs central.

---

## C. Designers (UX)

### Responsibilities

* Improve user experience.
* Convert requirements into usable designs.
* Create prototypes.
* Interact with users.

**Goal:** Build software that is easy and pleasant to use. 

---

## D. Software Engineers

### Responsibilities

* Implement requirements by writing code.
* Work with other engineers.

Important point:

Software engineers generally **implement the product**, while product decisions are usually made by product managers and business teams. 

---

## E. Engineering Manager (Project Manager)

### Responsibilities

* Organize work.
* Prioritize tasks.
* Coordinate teams.
* Communicate between management and developers.
* Resolve conflicts.

Think of the Engineering Manager as the person who **keeps the project moving smoothly.** 

---

## F. Sales Team

### Responsibilities

* Sell products to target customers.
* Collect customer feedback.
* Pass feedback to product, design, and engineering teams.

They act as the **voice of customers** in the organization. 

---

## G. Support Team

### Responsibilities

* Resolve customer problems.
* Report defects.
* Share customer feedback with engineering and product teams.

Support improves the product after release. 

---

## H. Data Scientists

### Responsibilities

* Analyze business and user data.
* Support decision making.
* Track sales.
* Understand user behavior.

Examples

* Marketing analytics
* Product usage analysis
* Sales analysis



---

## I. Ethics and Policy Specialists

### Responsibilities

* Privacy policies
* Software licenses
* Terms of service
* Legal compliance

Especially important for software that handles user data. 

---

# 3. Communication and Collaboration

## Conceptual Integrity

### Definition

**Conceptual Integrity** means **everyone on the team shares the same understanding of what is being built and why.**

Good communication ensures conceptual integrity.

Without conceptual integrity,

* different developers build different interpretations,
* resulting in inconsistent software.

# 5. Communication Principles

The lecture emphasizes that software engineering is **not only about writing code**.

Successful software development requires:

* Communication
* Coordination
* Documentation
* Feedback
* Shared understanding

These improve collaboration and reduce misunderstandings.

# High-Probability Comparisons

## Product Manager vs Software Engineer

| Product Manager               | Software Engineer               |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Decides product direction     | Implements requirements         |
| Focuses on users and business | Focuses on coding               |
| Coordinates across teams      | Collaborates within engineering |

---

## Marketing vs Sales

| Marketing                | Sales                |
| ------------------------ | -------------------- |
| Finds customers          | Sells to customers   |
| Market research          | Customer interaction |
| Identifies opportunities | Generates revenue    |

---

## Sales vs Support

| Sales                    | Support         |
| ------------------------ | --------------- |
| Before purchase          | After purchase  |
| Acquires customers       | Helps customers |
| Collects buying feedback | Resolves issues |

---

## Engineering Manager vs Product Manager

| Engineering Manager     | Product Manager                          |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Manages execution       | Defines product direction                |
| Coordinates engineering | Coordinates business, UX, and technology |
| Focuses on delivery     | Focuses on value and strategy            |
