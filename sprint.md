The correct answer is:

✅ **To evaluate the last sprint - team dynamics, processes, tools, etc.**

### Why?

A **Sprint Retrospective** is held **at the end of every sprint**, after the Sprint Review.

Its purpose is to reflect on **how the team worked**, not just **what they built**.

The team discusses:

* ✅ What went well?
* ✅ What didn't go well?
* ✅ What can be improved?
* ✅ Team collaboration
* ✅ Development process
* ✅ Tools and practices

The outcome is a set of **action items** to improve the next sprint.

#### ❌ To decide which work is to be done in the current sprint and how the chosen work will be done.

#### ❌ To prioritize the work for the development team.

This is done during **Product Backlog Refinement** (or by the **Product Owner** maintaining the Product Backlog).

---

#### ❌ Team demonstrates what they have completed in the given sprint.

This is the purpose of the **Sprint Review**.
